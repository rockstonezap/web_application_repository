Fundamentals of Computer Engineering

Overview Content

The Fundamentals of Computer Engineering course is designed to provide an understanding of computer engineering from both academic and professional perspectives. It equips students with foundational knowledge of the architecture of computers, how they and the infrastructure currently used in our daily lives communicate with each other, a perspective into the current market for computer engineers of various types along with details into the AI adoption while also introducing the diverse career opportunities available to computer engineers and their critical role in contemporary society.

The course is divided into two main parts. The first part focuses on theoretical foundations and is structured into three key blocks. The first block, Computer Engineering in Society, examines the historical background of the field, exploring its evolution from past to present and projecting its future. It also points out the discipline as both an academic pursuit and a professional career.

The second block, Information and Data, delves into the representation and organisation of information. It introduces fundamental concepts such as number systems, data coding, and models, alongside contemporary topics like Big Data, data protection, and the legal and ethical challenges surrounding information management.

The third block, Information Processing, Management, and Transmission addresses the structure and functionality of computer systems, including hardware, software, operating systems, and networks. It explores modern paradigms and technologies such as cloud computing, quantum computing, artificial intelligence, blockchain, robotics, IoT, and smart cities, highlighting their interconnected roles in modern computing landscapes.

The second part of the course transitions into the development of practical skills. Students are introduced to web development through two projects that involve the research into a cherry-picked topic from the list provided by the teacher and the creation of a web page on topics related to the course content. This practical component provides experience with soft skills such as teamwork, perseverance and communication. Objectives

  • Understand your societal value, and reflect on your future role as an engineer.
  • Learn the foundational principles of technological progress, with a historical perspective on its development.
  • Appreciate the significance of a human aspect in a professional environment, nurturing a commitment to provide proper service to users and do it for the common good.
  • Develop a global perspective of the field while exploring its various areas of knowledge, and identify diverse career paths based on your personal interests.
  • Gain insight into current computing paradigms, trends in data management, and learning the basics of web development.

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand and effectively use the terminology related to hardware and software in explaining or discussing the design, operation, and use of computer systems across various areas within the field of computer engineering.
  • Correlate key historical facts with new trends or evolutions in computers and computer science, as well as to explain and analyse future trends.
  • Explain the fundamentals and apply the different mechanisms for representing and organizing both numerical and non-numerical information within a computer system.
  • Explain the basic aspects of the fundamental areas of Computer Engineering: hardware, software and networks.
  • Be capable of creating a web page using HTML, CSS and Github Pages.
  • Research and adequately present, both orally and in writing, a computer project

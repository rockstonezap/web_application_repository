Computer Engineering Degree

Computer Engineering is a subject that provides principles from computer science, electronics, mathematics and humanities amongst other, to provide a comprehensive education on hardware/software engineering along with problem solving capabilities.

Mathematics and Humanities are added to the program for the student to more profoundly comprehend the landscape of the world, and be capable of adapting its technology to the usage requirements.

These subjects are taught in a 4-year program, with a total of 8 semesters, and a total of 240 ECTS credits.

The following tables will provide the information of the subjects, with a brief description of each one, among when they are taught and the area of study they belong to.

All subjects from this program are in the field of study of Engineering and Architecture.

Year 1

Subject Semester Description
Discrete Mathematics Sem 1 Equips the student to apply set theory and graph theory, use finite and modular arithmetic, employ logical and recursive methods for systematic reasoning, prove properties of infinite sets using mathematical induction, and define infinite sets through a finite set of recursive rules.
Mathematics Engineering I Sem 1 Equips the student with sufficient mathematical skills to tackle common problems in computer engineering, understanding their transversal nature and following a process of orientation, representation, decision-making, and action to arrive at a solution.
Introduction to Programming Sem 1 Trains students to solve problems algorithmically, fostering the algorithmic thinking required for a future computer engineer. This involves systematically analyzing problems, designing algorithmic steps for their resolution, and coding solutions according to basic computer science principles.
Fundamentals of Computer Engineering Sem 1 The course aims to provide students with a comprehensive understanding of Computer Engineering, including its goals, societal impact, and professional opportunities. It emphasizes the importance of ethical practice, technological history, current trends, and foundational knowledge in computing, data management, and web development.
Logic and Critical Thinking Sem 1 It provides the necessary foundation and key skills for the further development of actionable capabilities in the field of Big Data, along with technological knowledge.
Mathematics Engineering II Sem 2 The course aims to provide students with sufficient mathematical skills to address common problems in computer engineering, recognizing their interdisciplinary nature and following a structured process of orientation, representation, decision-making, and action to find solutions.
Electronics and Computer Technology Sem 2 The course aims to teach the fundamentals of electronics, focusing on their role in the physical operation of computers, peripherals, and communication systems, while introducing the principles of logical design for creating simple digital circuits. Key topics include the design and analysis of combinational and sequential systems, the study of simple analog circuits, an introduction to semiconductors, and the understanding of CMOS transistors.
The Company and its Processes Sem 2 This course introduces students to the business environment with a focus on the role of ICT in organizations, helping them develop critical thinking and a deeper understanding of business processes. It covers the evolution of businesses, turning ideas into viable projects, basic accounting and finance, ICT tools and systems, and managing key business processes to achieve organizational goals.
Object Oriented Programming Sem 2 The course focuses on mastering the analysis, design, implementation, and testing of software using Object-Oriented Programming (OOP) principles. Emphasis is placed on practical application, particularly during the implementation phase, with C# as the primary programming language to build robust and maintainable applications.
History of Science and Technology Sem 2 The course aims to provide students with a broad understanding of key historical events shaping their culture, emphasizing the impact of scientific development on modern history. It seeks to contextualize their profession within its historical evolution, highlighting its connections to philosophy, mathematics, and physics, and its progression through advancements in technology, machine manufacturing, and system construction.
Management of Personal Knowledge and Skills Annual The subject aims to promote personal maturity and self-awareness by helping students recognize habits for personal growth, understand the value of university culture, and reflect on the impact of their academic journey. Specific goals include fostering a deeper understanding of reality, teamwork, communication skills, personal development, the university’s mission, and the foundations of creative leadership.

Year 2

Subject Semester Description
Data Structures and Algorithms Sem 3 This course aims to provide students with both theoretical and practical knowledge, along with the skills to master algorithmic techniques for developing efficient applications while fostering critical thinking. It also emphasizes humanistic aspects, focusing on: anthropological objectives, such as encouraging innovation, truth-seeking, social engagement, and a vocation for service; epistemological goals, highlighting the fundamental principles and ultimate purposes of data structure modeling techniques; ethical considerations, underscoring the human orientation of technical operations with a focus on quality, cost optimization, and serving the common good; and purpose-driven objectives, promoting the creation of models that benefit humanity and contribute to the greater good by carefully considering what data to include and its intended purpose.
Computer Architecture and Organisation Sem 3 This course aims to provide an understanding of computer hardware, including its components, performance criteria, and principles of low-level application development. Students will learn to analyze and evaluate system performance and select appropriate systems for specific needs. Key objectives include assembly language programming, analyzing Von Neumann architecture, optimizing memory access with cache techniques, and enhancing CPU performance using pipelines and parallelism.
Analysis and Design of Information Systems Sem 3 This course focuses on teaching students the methodologies and tools required for designing information systems, emphasizing the analysis of information flow, proper data storage, system states, and internal design. Students will learn to identify and structure the necessary information for system operation using modeling languages, analyze and represent system behavior with modeling tools, define use cases by exploring various scenarios, and map out the information flows required during the conception and lifecycle of a system or software product.
Web Development Sem 3 The primary goal of this course is to equip students with the knowledge needed to develop front-end web applications using proper structures, market-relevant designs, and device adaptability. Students will learn to create website structures in HTML5 that meet modern design and accessibility standards, apply CSS to develop visually appealing and responsive layouts, and utilize the Bootstrap framework for front-end development. Additionally, the course introduces JavaScript for adding interactivity, the JQuery library to simplify JavaScript usage, and explores search engine optimization (SEO) criteria to enhance website visibility.
Projects I Sem 4 This course equips students with the skills to work in teams to solve complex problems by identifying social issues that can be addressed with computer solutions. Students will research these problems, define projects, design and build software applications to meet requirements, and document and professionally present their results. The course emphasizes teamwork in engineering projects and encourages reflection on the societal implications and impact of their work.
Statistics Sem 4 The Statistics course aims to teach students descriptive techniques and data analysis to better understand the world. It covers descriptive statistics, probability calculations, and statistical distributions. Students will learn to estimate population parameters (e.g., mean, variance) for sample comparisons, perform hypothesis testing, and conduct linear regression analysis.
Databases Sem 4 This course on Database Management Systems (DBMS) is a fundamental component of Computer Engineering, aiming to provide students with essential knowledge in database design, query languages, updates, implementation, and administration. Students will develop the ability to adapt data models to meet DBMS requirements while understanding how information levels affect system integrity, availability, and confidentiality. It emphasizes the creative process of database development, encouraging students to see their scientific endeavors as a reflection of human curiosity and to engage with broader social, political, and economic questions. Key objectives include mastering DBMS concepts, implementing logical and physical database models, performing SQL operations, and working with NoSQL databases.
Operating Systems Sem 4 The main goal of this course is to introduce the nature and features of modern operating systems, focusing on their components, structure, and operational mechanisms. Specific objectives include understanding the general structure of contemporary operating systems, learning concurrent programming (POSIX), understanding virtual memory management, and studying the interaction between system hardware and the operating system.
Software Engineering I Sem 4 This course aims to provide students with a comprehensive understanding of software development by balancing the integration of people, material resources, and intellectual resources to create projects that meet client needs, whether explicitly stated or implied, while adhering to constraints of time, quality, and service. While traditionally focused on teaching techniques and tools to develop functional and client-satisfying software, this approach has often fallen short by either neglecting the human element or prioritizing economic objectives over quality work. The course emphasizes the importance of well-executed work, ensuring that methodologies, techniques, and tools are used not only effectively but also ethically, fostering growth and integrating human values into the software development process.
Anthropology and Social Responsibility Annual This course aims to help students explore their identity and humanity by addressing questions like “Who am I?” and “What is the human being?” It encourages them to recognize the ontological dignity of all people, live consistently with this awareness, and become transformative agents in the world. Students will identify global social challenges and engage with concepts such as corporate social responsibility, solidarity, human rights, and the common good, developing a social commitment to contribute meaningfully as students and future professionals in computer engineering. The course also fosters intellectual and emotional maturity, critical understanding of contemporary anthropological realities, and the application of technical knowledge for the betterment of society, distinguishing between means, ends, and results.

Year 3

Subject Semester Description
Artificial Intelligence I Sem 5 This course aims to provide students with a comprehensive understanding of the fundamental principles of Artificial Intelligence (AI), exploring its dual role as a science replicating human cognition (strong AI) and as an engineering discipline developing intelligent systems to complement human activities (weak AI). It addresses the philosophical, ethical, and social implications of AI, including its potential for consciousness and its impact on society through technologies like social networks. Students will reflect on AI from scientific and anthropological perspectives, understand its scientific foundations, and learn techniques for developing intelligent systems and agents, preparing them for advanced studies in AI and knowledge engineering.
Software Engineering II Sem 5 Software Engineering II aims to deepen students’ understanding of the complexities of software system development by focusing on organizing material and human resources for sustainable and high-quality software. The course emphasizes defining and managing software products based on critical quality attributes, understanding and applying agile methodologies like Scrum and DevOps, and transforming requirements into quantifiable work structures. It highlights the evolutionary nature of software and the importance of maintainability, managing product quality through metrics and SMART objectives, and understanding various software testing approaches. Additionally, it complements parallel courses by covering integration strategies, continuous deployment environments, and software reuse, particularly in web development frameworks.
Software Development and Integration Sem 5 The main objective of the course is to study the different strategies for software integration in the field of Service Oriented Architecture, understood as a software architecture for the development of applications that, based on components in charge of offering a certain service, implementing business processes.
Human Computer Interaction Sem 5 This course focuses on applying methodological knowledge to the life cycle of graphical user interfaces, emphasizing human factors and accessibility to create user-centered designs. Students will learn to understand psychological factors in human-computer interaction, develop user-centered designs through real-life case studies, utilize interface modeling tools, and apply techniques to reduce subjectivity in user-centered system modeling.
Computational Complexity Sem 5 The Computational Complexity course aims to provide students with a solid understanding of the theoretical foundations of algorithmic time complexity and the skills to calculate the complexity of specific algorithms. It focuses on methods for analyzing and designing algorithms to solve generic problems like sorting and searching efficiently. By the end of the course, students will be equipped to evaluate the computational complexity of their solutions and choose the best implementation strategies. Key topics include the theoretical basis and historical challenges of computational complexity, such as the “P=NP?” problem, designing algorithms with minimal computational cost, and applying optimization techniques like Divide and Conquer, recursive algorithms, greedy algorithms, dynamic programming, and backtracking.
Systems Administration Sem 6 This course focuses on deepening students’ understanding of the evolution of operating systems and their role in hardware and software development. It covers the properties, capabilities, and suitability of various systems through a comparative analysis of their features, particularly file management and user administration. The course addresses integration and communication challenges between operating systems and their role as platforms for vendor-provided and custom software solutions. Students will gain the skills to select and install appropriate systems based on service requirements and environment constraints, while also exploring virtualization infrastructure and service models to make informed decisions about on-premise and cloud architectures.
Advanced Databases Sem 6 This course focuses on the principles, structure, and key features of distributed database systems, covering their design, functionality, and management. Students will learn to address challenges related to the interconnection of heterogeneous, federated database systems, select and administer various database management systems, and plan for contingencies like backups and data recovery, evaluating the impact of potential failures. Additionally, the course emphasizes creating applications using procedural languages for databases, including web-based systems, and utilizing modular structures, control structures, exception handling, and process triggers.
Networks and Distributed Systems Sem 6 This course aims to introduce students to the existence and functioning of communication networks, exploring their past uses and future potential with a focus on distributed systems. It examines key standards underpinning TCP/IP architecture, including wired (IEEE 802.3 Ethernet) and wireless (IEEE 802.11) technologies. Emphasis is placed on a practical approach to understanding, designing, and managing communication networks tailored to different scenarios, with careful consideration of security requirements.
Projects II Sem 6 This course aims to equip students with the skills to apply their knowledge in a team-based software project, from conception to execution, including documentation and oral presentation. Students will identify social problems that can be addressed with software solutions, critically analyze these issues, and design and build applications using appropriate methodologies and technologies. The course emphasizes teamwork, risk management, effective communication in English, and creating high-quality technical documentation. It also encourages students to reflect on the societal impact of their projects, contributing to the common good.
Statistical Learning and Data Mining Sem 6 This course focuses on teaching techniques and methodologies for extracting relevant information from various data types to design solutions that support decision-making and enable descriptive and predictive tasks. Students will learn to explore datasets using statistical and visualization techniques, apply predictive classification and regression methods, utilize descriptive techniques like association analysis and clustering, and implement data mining methodologies such as CRISP-DM. The course also emphasizes understanding, selecting, and appropriately applying statistical learning and data mining techniques.
Artificial Intelligence II Sem 6 This course focuses on sub symbolic intelligent systems, exploring their role in modeling cognitive processes and their technological applications in Deep Learning. It covers key Artificial Intelligence techniques, including connectionist models like neural networks (supervised and unsupervised) for classification and prediction problems, and biologically inspired models like genetic algorithms for optimization. Students will study the methodology for developing connectionist systems to solve real-world industrial and business problems, gain insight into Deep Learning as a prevalent AI solution, and explore the biological foundations and practical applications of genetic algorithms.
Professional Ethics and Deontology Annual This course aims to explore ethics as a discipline focused on human fulfillment and happiness through freedom, love, and responsibility. It equips students with fundamental ethical principles and values to guide personal, social, and professional actions, promoting moral discernment and the pursuit of the common good. Students are encouraged to recognize their role as ethical agents, develop critical thinking, and understand the importance of moral virtues in transforming both personal and societal existence. The course emphasizes reasoning ethically, applying ethical principles to real-life challenges, avoiding a mechanical approach to ethics, and evaluating the ethical implications of professional projects. Ultimately, it fosters an integrated approach that combines theoretical, technical, and practical dimensions of ethical and philosophical thinking.

Year 4

Subject Semester Description
Quality Management Sem 7 To enhance the education of future engineers by incorporating quality as a fundamental dimension in software engineering. This will enable students to identify and use metrics to produce high-quality products, execute projects that meet required standards, and adapt to advanced management systems.
High Performance Computing Sem 7 This course focuses on studying current technical solutions, such as parallel, concurrent, and real-time programming, to address computationally complex societal challenges. It covers understanding societal problems requiring supercomputing, familiarizing with widely used supercomputer topologies, designing energy-efficient algorithms to tackle the sustainability challenges of supercomputers, and exploring supercomputing models like MPI and GPGPU. The course aims to equip students with the knowledge and tools to leverage supercomputing for societal excellence.
The God Question in the Digital Era Sem 7 This course explores the meaning of human existence by analyzing the concept of Mystery, examining common answers through reason and faith, and studying the phenomenology of religious experience. It also clarifies the perception of sacred time and space within the secular world and investigates the historical aspects of Christianity and the Church as responses to contemporary human needs.
Security Sem 7 This course focuses on understanding risk analysis and management methodologies as key aspects of Information Systems Security, emphasizing the analysis of organizational business processes, supporting information assets, and potential threats and vulnerabilities. It highlights the importance of incorporating security considerations from the inception of software projects, including security requirements analysis and activities to ensure compliance. Key topics include confidentiality, integrity, and availability, addressing both accidental risks and malicious actions, alongside intellectual property rights. The course also explores the technical, organizational, and legal implications of information security through case studies and promotes a comprehensive approach to managing technical and professional resources throughout the planning, design, development, implementation, and maintenance phases of Information Systems Security.

Knowledge Engineering Sem 8 This course focuses on studying and applying symbolic reasoning techniques in Artificial Intelligence (AI) to solve domain-specific problems using general problem-solving methods. It emphasizes two core elements: problem representation and solution acquisition. Key topics include symbolic AI definitions and techniques, symbolic processing models (especially state-space-based), and search algorithms such as uninformed, heuristic, adversarial, and constraint-based searches. Students will apply these algorithms to real-world problems, like video games and route optimization, by programming practical solutions.
Final Project Sem 8 The Final Degree Project (PFG) provides students with the opportunity to complete a comprehensive computer project, integrating knowledge from their Computer Engineering studies while adopting an engineering and research-oriented perspective. Students will develop technical projects, prepare reports, draft technical papers, and present their work orally. The course emphasizes project planning using appropriate methodologies and expanding knowledge through research and consultation of relevant bibliographic sources.

Planning and Managing IT Projects Annual This course emphasizes the transition from viewing Computer Systems Management as an art to treating it as an engineering discipline, focusing on effective planning and project management. It highlights the importance of team management and human relationships in achieving successful project outcomes, while aligning projects with client needs and fostering personal and professional growth. Students will learn standard IT project management methods, techniques for defining and managing projects, and skills for planning, controlling, and monitoring. The course also includes applying these principles and competencies to a real-world project.
